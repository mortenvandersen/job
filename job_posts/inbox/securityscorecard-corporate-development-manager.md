# SecurityScorecard — Corporate Development Manager

## Job Description

About SecurityScorecard:
SecurityScorecard is the global leader in cybersecurity ratings, with over 12 million companies continuously rated, operating in 64 countries. Founded in 2013 by security and risk experts Dr. Alex Yampolskiy and Sam Kassoumeh and funded by world-class investors, SecurityScorecard’s patented rating technology is used by over 25,000 organizations for self-monitoring, third-party risk management, board reporting, and cyber insurance underwriting; making all organizations more resilient by allowing them to easily find and fix cybersecurity risks across their digital footprint.
Headquartered in New York City, our culture has been recognized by Inc Magazine as a "Best Workplace,” by Crain’s NY as a "Best Places to Work in NYC," and as one of the 10 hottest SaaS startups in New York for two years in a row. Most recently, SecurityScorecard was named to Fast Company’s annual list of the World’s Most Innovative Companies for 2023 and to the Achievers 50 Most Engaged Workplaces in 2023 award recognizing “forward-thinking employers for their unwavering commitment to employee engagement.” SecurityScorecard is proud to be funded by world-class investors including Silver Lake Waterman, Moody’s, Sequoia Capital, GV and Riverwood Capital.
About the Role:
The Corporate Development Manager will help drive SecurityScorecard’s inorganic growth strategy, supporting M&A, strategic investments, and strategic alliances that accelerate distribution, expand product value, and strengthen our position as the trusted cyber risk layer across the ecosystem.
This role is highly cross-functional and execution-oriented. You’ll build conviction through market and competitive research, formulate M&A swimlanes, map market segments, identify targets, develop strategic theses and business cases, coordinate diligence and integration planning, and help structure partnerships that create measurable outcomes. You’ll partner closely with Finance, Legal, Product, Engineering, and GTM.
What You'll Do:
Corporate development strategy & pipeline
Build and maintain a prioritized pipeline of acquisition, investment, and strategic alliance opportunities aligned to our product and go-to-market roadmap.Develop a crisp point-of-view on “why now / why us” for priority themes (e.g., supply chain risk, threat intel, security & risk operations, cyber insurance).Build relationships with founders, investors, and bankers; manage inbound opportunities with clear fit/no-fit rationale.
Market, product, and GTM diligence
Lead strategic diligence: market sizing and structure, competitive landscape, customer needs, product differentiation, and route-to-market.Partner with Product and Engineering to evaluate technical fit at a high level (architecture, data, integrations, security posture) and translate findings into decisions.Coordinate with Sales/GTM leaders to assess commercial fit, co-sell potential, and distribution synergies.
Deal process execution 
Manage end-to-end deal process mechanics: NDAs, process timelines, diligence trackers, workstream owners, readouts, and decision memos.Coordinate with Finance on valuation inputs and assumptions, ensuring the strategic narrative and diligence findings translate into decision-ready recommendations.Support negotiation preparation (terms, risks, deal structure) alongside Legal and executive sponsors.
Strategic alliances & partnerships
Help identify, structure, and launch strategic alliances (referral, reseller, OEM, platform, data partnerships).Create partner value propositions and joint plans (what we’re building together, who sells, how we measure success).Establish operating rhythms and success metrics to ensure alliances scale beyond a launch announcement.
Integration planning & value capture
Partner with functional leaders to define Day 1 / Day 30 / Day 100 integration plans, owners, and milestones.Track post-close execution and value capture; surface risks early and drive resolution across teams.For partnerships, help ensure activation (enablement, messaging, pipeline motions, product hooks) and measurable impact.
Executive communication
Produce concise memos, IC/board-ready decks, and stakeholder updates that synthesize complex diligence into clear decisions.Bring clarity: what we learned, what matters, what to do next.
Required Qualifications:
2+ years of experience in corporate development, investment banking, private equity/VC, strategy, consulting, or a similar role with exposure to M&A and/or strategic partnership execution.Demonstrated ability to run cross-functional processes and drive outcomes in a fast-paced environment.Strong strategic thinking and structured problem-solving (market + competitive analysis, thesis development, synergy articulation).Excellent communication skills, written and verbal, with a track record of producing decision-ready materials.AI fluency: practical experience using AI and modern productivity tools to improve work quality and throughput (research, synthesis, process automation, documentation).High ownership, sound judgment, and comfort operating with ambiguity.
Preferred Qualifications:
Experience in Cyber Security and/or B2B SaaS (bonus for third-party risk, threat intel, security operations, cyber insurance).Experience supporting integration planning and value capture post-close.Experience helping build or scale strategic alliances (co-sell, channel, OEM, platform, data partnerships).Familiarity with financial concepts (unit economics, valuation drivers, deal structures) even if Finance owns modeling.
