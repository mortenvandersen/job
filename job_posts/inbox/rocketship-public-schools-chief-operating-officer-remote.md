# Rocketship Public Schools — Chief Operating Officer (Remote)

## Job Description

Remote Bay Area/California, Nashville, Milwaukee/Chicago area, or Washington, DC/DMV 
About Rocketship Public SchoolsRocketship schools are top tier public elementary schools known for academic results and a holistic approach to personalized learning. The Rocketship network has successfully launched 22 elementary schools across the country since first opening in the Bay Area over eighteen years ago.
Rocketship Public Schools' non-profit network of public elementary charter schools propels student achievement, develops exceptional educators, and intentionally partners with parents to enable high-quality public schools to thrive in their community. Rocketship Public Schools is a collective of parents, teachers, leaders, and students working together to transform the future for underserved communities across our country. At Rocketship Public Schools, we are unleashing potential.
Position DescriptionRocketship is at an inflection point following a period of significant organizational change, including regionalization and efforts to better integrate core functions across the network. As the organization looks to its next phase of growth, the Chief Operating Officer will play a critical role in strengthening execution, building more connected systems, and ensuring consistency across regions.
This is an enterprise leadership role responsible for strengthening and leading the core operating systems that power internal execution and alignment across Rocketship's national network including human resources, technology, data systems, analytics, and school operations. The COO will lead an organization of 30+ team members, including 5 senior leaders. In this role, the COO will ensure that talent systems, operational processes, and technology work together to give school leaders the staff, support, and data they need to continuously elevate student outcomes.
The COO will serve as a key strategic partner to the CEO and executive team, translating priorities into action and ensuring follow-through on the organization's most important work while helping build the structure, systems, and leadership needed to sustain Rocketship's impact over time.
Rocketship's ability to deliver strong academic outcomes depends on having the right people, reliable systems, and consistent operations across all schools. This role exists to strengthen that foundation—so school leaders can spend less time managing operational challenges and more time focused on teaching and learning.
In the first 12 months, success will include strengthening talent acquisition and workforce planning, improving alignment and consistency across regions, and establishing clearer systems for decision-making, accountability, and operational performance.
