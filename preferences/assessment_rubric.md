# Job Fit Assessment Rubric

_Use this rubric when assessing any job post. Score each dimension, flag deal-breakers
immediately, then produce a weighted overall score and recommendation._

---

## Step 1 — Deal-Breaker Check (Automatic Disqualify)

Run these first. If any applies, **stop and reject** — don't score further.

| # | Check | Flag |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Role is explicitly quota-carrying / commission-based | Hard no |
| 2 | Role is part-time or fractional | Hard no |
| 3 | In-office or hybrid outside San Francisco Bay Area | Hard no |
| 4 | Requires European/EMEA working hours | Hard no |
| 5 | Company is in clear contraction / decline | Hard no |
| 6 | Company has fewer than 50 total employees | Hard no |
| 7 | Base salary ceiling is below $200K (when stated) | Hard no |
| 8 | Industry exclusion (oil, tobacco, gambling, etc.) | Hard no |

> **Note on location**: NY, NJ, Chicago, London, or other non-Bay Area locations are
> an automatic disqualifier if the role is in-office or hybrid. Remote-first roles
> anywhere in the US are fine. Always check carefully — many job posts say "SF Bay Area"
> in the search filter but specify a different HQ city in the body.

---

## Step 2 — Fit Scoring (1–5 per dimension)

### Dimension A: Role Type Fit (weight: 30%)

Does the role match the target functional areas?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Core target role: Corp Strategy, BizOps/StratOps, Corp Dev, GM/Business Lead |
| 4 | Adjacent and strong: Chief of Staff, Head of New Markets, Strategy & Planning |
| 3 | Partial fit: Role has strong strategic elements but leans too far into one function (e.g., heavy analytics, heavy PM, heavy marketing) |
| 2 | Stretch: Role is a significant departure from target — e.g., pure product, pure ops execution, program management |
| 1 | Poor fit: Functional mismatch — e.g., engineering manager, sales leader, finance controller |

**Watch for**: "Growth" roles that are really marketing. "Strategy" roles that are really analytics or reporting. "Program Manager" roles that are project coordination, not strategy.

**On "Business Development" titles**: These span a wide range. VP or Director-level BD is often a strategic partnerships, new markets, or corp dev role — always score on the actual responsibilities described, not the title alone. Only treat as a deal-breaker if the JD explicitly mentions quotas, revenue targets, or commission structure. Junior BD titles (Manager, Associate) are more likely to be pipeline/sales roles and warrant closer scrutiny of the JD.

---

### Dimension B: Industry Fit (weight: 20%)

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | EdTech (Higher Ed, K–12, CTE, corporate L&D, healthcare education) |
| 4 | AI/ML companies, consumer tech, companies with a meaningful education product or unit, sustainability/climate tech |
| 3 | General tech/SaaS, fintech, healthcare (non-education), enterprise software |
| 2 | Non-tech industry with strong role fit (e.g., media, retail, CPG) |
| 1 | Industry with no personal connection or interest |

---

### Dimension C: Company Stage & Health (weight: 15%)

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Growth-stage startup (100–1000 people), strong revenue growth, clear PMF, VC or PE backed |
| 4 | Established tech company with a growing product line / BU; or earlier-stage startup with strong signals |
| 3 | Large enterprise (stable, not declining); or mid-stage company with unclear trajectory |
| 2 | Large enterprise in a legacy/commoditized market; or company with concerning signals |
| 1 | Contracting business, declining market, or financial distress signals |

---

### Dimension D: Scope & Seniority Fit (weight: 20%)

Does the role give meaningful ownership and leverage Morten's operating experience?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Real ownership: P&L, business unit, strategic agenda, team — reports to C-suite or VP |
| 4 | Significant scope: leads cross-functional initiatives, shapes strategy, manages a team or key function |
| 3 | Moderate scope: executes strategy rather than defines it; limited team ownership; primarily supports a leader |
| 2 | Limited scope: individual contributor in a large function; mostly analytical or coordination work |
| 1 | Below experience level: junior scope, no strategic mandate |

**Note**: IC roles are acceptable if scope and impact are strong (score them on impact, not headcount).

---

### Dimension E: Compensation Fit (weight: 15%)

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Base $200K+, strong equity (RSUs or meaningful options), bonus — total comp clearly above $250K |
| 4 | Base $200K+ with bonus; or base slightly below with strong equity upside narrative |
| 3 | Base at or near $200K but limited upside signals; or range straddles the floor |
| 2 | Range tops out near $200K; risk of landing below floor |
| 1 | Range clearly below $200K |

> If no comp is listed, note it and don't penalize — but flag for follow-up.

---

### Dimension F: Career Trajectory Value (weight: 0% — qualitative only)

Not scored numerically, but always comment on: does this role move Morten toward a GM
or CEO track, or is it a lateral/sideways move? Is it building new toolkit (corp dev,
P&L, new industry) or reinforcing existing strengths only?

---

## Step 3 — Role Fit Score (weighted)

This score measures how well the role aligns with Morten's goals, preferences, and career direction.

```
Role Fit Score = (A × 0.30) + (B × 0.20) + (C × 0.15) + (D × 0.20) + (E × 0.15)
```

| Role Fit Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 4.5 – 5.0 | **Strong Yes** — prioritize, tailor carefully |
| 3.5 – 4.4 | **Yes with flags** — pursue, but note what to probe in interviews |
| 2.5 – 3.4 | **Marginal** — only if pipeline is thin or industry is Tier 1 EdTech |
| Below 2.5 | **No** — not worth the effort regardless of candidate fit |

---

## Step 4 — Candidate Fit Score (1–5)

This score measures how well Morten's background matches what the role requires. Assessed independently from role fit — a role can be a great fit for Morten but a poor fit for his profile on paper, or vice versa.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Meets all stated requirements including hard requirements. Resume passes ATS cleanly. Hiring manager sees a near-perfect match on paper. |
| 4 | Meets all hard requirements. One or two soft/preferred qualifications missing or partial. Strong application with minor framing work needed. |
| 3 | Meets core requirements but has one meaningful gap recruiters will notice — e.g. industry experience, specific credential, deal count. Passable but needs strong framing or warm introduction. |
| 2 | Missing one hard requirement that is likely to be enforced at screening (specific industry years, required degree type, specific credential). Will typically be filtered without a referral. |
| 1 | Missing multiple hard requirements. Only viable with extraordinary access (direct C-suite connection, etc.). |

**What to assess for candidate fit:**
- Does he meet the experience level and years-of-experience requirements?
- Does he have the required domain/industry background (or a strong adjacent case)?
- Are there credential requirements (specific degree, certification) he doesn't meet?
- Does his functional background match what the role actually needs day-to-day?
- Would a standard ATS or recruiter screen pass his resume?

---

## Step 5 — Recommendation

The recommendation is a holistic judgment that takes **both scores** into account:

| Role Fit | Candidate Fit | Likely recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| High (4.5+) | High (4–5) | Strong Yes — apply immediately |
| High (4.5+) | Moderate (3) | Yes — apply with strong framing or warm intro |
| High (4.5+) | Low (1–2) | Referral only — cold apply is unlikely to clear screening |
| Moderate (3.5–4.4) | High (4–5) | Yes with flags — strong application, but role has concerns |
| Moderate (3.5–4.4) | Moderate (3) | Conditional — only if pipeline is thin |
| Low (<3.5) | Any | Marginal or No |

---

## Step 6 — Assessment Output Format

When assessing a job post, always produce every section below in this order:

```
## Fit Assessment: <Role Title> — <Company>

**Deal-breakers**: None / [list any that apply — stop here if any are hard nos]

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| A. Role Type | X/5 | ... |
| B. Industry | X/5 | ... |
| C. Company Stage | X/5 | ... |
| D. Scope & Seniority | X/5 | ... |
| E. Compensation | X/5 | ... |

**Role Fit Score**: X.X / 5 — [Strong Yes / Yes with flags / Marginal / No]
**Candidate Fit Score**: X / 5 — [Excellent / Strong / Moderate / Weak / Poor]
**Recommendation**: [Holistic call combining both scores — e.g. "Apply via referral", "Strong Yes — apply immediately", "Referral only", "Skip"]

**Trajectory value**: [Does this role move toward GM/CEO track, build new toolkit,
or is it lateral? Be specific.]

**Gaps & flags** — requirements Morten does not clearly meet:
- 🔴 [Hard gap]: [specific requirement from JD vs. what's missing]
- 🟡 [Soft gap / yellow flag]: [partial match or uncertainty]
(Omit this section only if there are genuinely no meaningful gaps)

**Strongest angles — what to emphasize if applying**:
- [Specific experience or credential that directly maps to a key requirement]
- [...]
```

## Notes From Historical Ratings (patterns to calibrate against)

Based on the initial set of ~29 rated roles, the following patterns held:

**Consistent 5s**: Corp Strategy Manager, Head of BizOps, Corp Dev roles at SF Bay Area
tech companies (Retool, Adobe, NVIDIA, Block, Persona, Moloco). These share: clear
strategy mandate, SF location, $200K+ comp, tech/AI industry, and real scope.

**Note on the historical dataset**: The ~29 roles in the reference set were already
pre-filtered — lower-scoring posts had been removed before rating. So the absence of
1–3 scores reflects curation, not rating inflation. The patterns above still hold for
calibrating within the 4–5 range.

**Common calibration errors to avoid**:
- NY/NJ-based hybrid roles were rated as 5s (Scholastic, NYU) — location should have
  cut these significantly
- "Head of Growth" or "Growth Lead" roles skew marketing, not strategy — check scope
  before scoring high
- "Program Manager" titles often lack strategic mandate — check scope carefully
- "VP Business Development" is not automatically a sales role — read the JD before
  flagging; it may be corp dev or new markets under a different label
- Comp below floor was sometimes overlooked — always check the range ceiling, not just
  the floor
